The new look bluechipholidays.co.uk
 
The new website has now been live for almost a month and our analysis indicates that guests are experiencing some positive changes to their holiday searches. In the time that the new website has been live we’ve seen our bounce rate decrease by 52.1%. That means that over 81% of visitors view more than one page on our new site, compared to 61% on the previous site.

Our guests are also looking at almost 50% more pages per visit and the average session duration has increased by 56 seconds

The new website has also generated a 314% increase in email sign-ups, compared to the average figures for the past six months, helping us to grow our email database to over 133,000.

These are all positive metrics relating directly to the change in website, but we do not want to stop here. We recognise that there are some tweaks to be made and we have a program for making these changes moving forwards. With the new website we now have a platform that will allow us to make much quicker strides forwards and react to changing market conditions.

The market is changing at a rate of knots; demand this year has followed the last minute pattern that we expected, and spurred on by the weather the late market has been huge. Where there has been the need for a promotion, the new discount engine has allowed us to place strategic discounts for property types and areas that have needed that extra push.

There have been further changes in guests’ behaviour, with an increase in mobile and tablet traffic of 49% compared to the same period last year.

This July was our best ever July for created bookings; we created 25% more bookings than July 2013, with an average booking value increase of 38%, highlighting the late demand in the market.

Welcome packs
 
We enjoy reading the lovely reviews we receive from guests, especially as it gives us the opportunity to improve what we do. One thing that comes up time again is the inclusion of welcome packs in Blue Chip properties. After a long, often tiring journey, the one thing that’s sure to get any holiday off to a great start is a bottle of wine, a vase of fresh flowers or a basket full of delicious, local treats.

“We loved the welcoming cake and wine which was well received after the journey with kids”

“Lovely welcoming touches were the vase of fresh flowers on the table and milk in the fridge, along with coffee, teabags and sugar so we could have a hot drink on arrival after a very long and difficult journey”

“The owner was very helpful and the welcome pack was an unexpected bonus”

As a minimum, a small supply of coffee, tea, milk and sugar should be provided. Any little extra touches, such as homemade cakes, locally sourced apple juice or beautiful fresh flowers, will be greatly appreciated and will be one of the things your guests remember about their stay.

 

Gas Safety
 

If you have a gas supply to your property, you must have an annual gas safety check by a gas safe registered engineer. If applicable, don’t forget to send a copy of the Gas Safe Certificate through to our quality team for our records. We will shortly be conducting a file audit, so one of our team will be in touch if any of your documents are missing.
